Toss

It was crucial – the pitch was wet, and it was obvious that winning the toss and bowling first would be an advantage. The ball was gripping, bouncing, but the track was slow and difficult to bat on early in the morning. We won the toss and put the opposition in to bat first. Honestly, a good toss to win.

Bowling

As a new team, we are doing a lot of things right, and one of them is knowing our roles and delivering on those expectations so far. Let’s keep building on this.

Govind and Krishna provided a great start, and our fielders did a fantastic job supporting them and all the other bowlers. Akhil and Ansh made sure we kept their batsmen under pressure and kept getting wickets at regular intervals – we never let the opposition off the hook. Rushi bowled his 4 overs and dismantled the opposition by grabbing 3 wickets from the other end. Then our fast bowlers – Krishna, Govind, and Akhil – finished up the tail. Oh, by the way, Akshay also bowled one over and grabbed a wicket to his name. The variety of our bowling unit is immense – we even had a leg spinner in Aniket waiting on the sidelines if needed. Thanks for everything you did, Aniket, and I am sure your time will come soon.

I do want to call out that our field placement is improving as we bowled our 20 overs in the given time – even earlier – so we are starting to do a few things right, while still having room for improvement.

Batting

The job was half done with the way our bowlers bowled. Our discussion in the huddle was simple: don’t give them opportunities to catch, play everything on the ground, and punish the loose balls – and the game is ours.

Credit to the opposition team for bagging 2 of our top batsmen for less than 15 runs. Aiyaz bhai went in at number 4 and was looking good with Aryan. But soon, the 3rd wicket fell at 35. Batting was not easy on this track. Aryan and Ansh played very sensibly, knocking the ball around, running hard, and punishing everything in their range. We were under a little pressure at 35-3, but Ansh and Aryan saw us through. Great batting performance from them.

Best Moments & Good Points

  • Govind and Krishna’s opening spell set the tone with great support from the fielders.
  • Akhil and Ansh kept the pressure on and took wickets at regular intervals.
  • Rushi’s 3-wicket haul dismantled the opposition’s middle order.
  • Akshay chipped in with a wicket in his over; Aniket ready as leg spinner backup.
  • Field placements and over-rate much improved – 20 overs bowled within time.
  • Aryan and Ansh’s sensible batting partnership under pressure.
  • Local players giving 110% and supporting the team’s undefeated run.

Areas to Improve

  • Continue working on field placements and adapting quickly to match situations.
  • Keep the intensity high in the field and support bowlers with sharp fielding.
  • Maintain focus in the batting – minimize risky shots and capitalize on loose deliveries.
  • As a team, let’s keep finding ways to support players making extra efforts (travel, commitments, etc.).

Final Words

Overall, a good victory heading into the bye week while being undefeated. Enjoy the week off and keep working hard. Our next game is against the Pacers, who are playing a strong brand of cricket themselves and will put our bowling, batting, and fielding to the test.
